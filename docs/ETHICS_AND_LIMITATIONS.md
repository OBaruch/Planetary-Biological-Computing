# Ethics And Limitations

GAIA-1 is a neuron-ready planetary simulation prototype. This MVP runs on the Cortical Labs CL SDK Simulator and does not use real neurons.

It does not demonstrate:

- biological learning;
- consciousness;
- sentience;
- thought;
- emotion;
- suffering;
- causal biological response to stimulation.

The stimulation intents are recorded as architecture artifacts for future integration. In simulator mode, they should not be interpreted as causing meaningful neural behavior.

The channel groups and decoded actions are visualization metaphors. They are not neuroscience claims.

The project is educational, experimental, artistic, and technical. Any future connection to biological neural systems through CL1 or Cortical Cloud should require review of stimulation safety, data handling, experiment purpose, consent/procurement context, and public communication.

Public demos should keep these phrases visible:

- "Simulator mode: no real neurons are connected."
- "Planetary signals are encoded into stimulation intents."
- "Simulated spikes are decoded into planetary actions."
- "Designed for future CL1/Cortical Cloud deployment."
